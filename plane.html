Our Planes

Pioneering the future of UAV technology.

ARES

Meet Ares, a high-performance UAV built for the UAS Challenge 2024. With a 2.2-meter wingspan and weighing just 5 kg, it delivers a 35-minute flight time and autonomous features like takeoff, landing, waypoint navigation, and payload drop. Ares is constructed with a carbon fiber body and EPP wings for durability and water resistance. Its modular design enables tool-free assembly in under 2 minutes. Capable of taking off in under 10 meters, it can carry up to 8 kg and cruises efficiently at 14 meters per second.

SKYWALKER

Built in 2017, a recent addition to the Team's plane collection. Designed for the AUVSI-SUAS competition, the plane has an endurance of 30-40 minutes of completely autonomous operation of object detection and classification, interoperability, waypoint navigation, flight, take-off and landing. It has a take-off gross weight of 7.5Kgs with a cruise speed of 12 m/s.

SANDY 2.0

A variant of Sandy with the same role as Sandy 1.0 but with twin motors and a camera mounted at the front to test the image capture features and is one of the finest planes built in the team's history.

SANDY

A relatively small plane used for testing of individual components. It was our first test dummy for autonomous flying and has provided excellent results to proceed autonomy to bigger plane.

SMOKEY

Built in 2015, this plane was the first to have the capability of an autonomous system ingrained within. Designed for AUVSI competition in 2015.

SAE PLANE

Built in 2015, this plane represented our team in SAE-2015 competition. A stable, robust and payload-efficient plane, it won 7th place in design and 12th place overall.
